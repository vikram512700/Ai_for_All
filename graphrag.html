GraphRAG & Knowledge Graphs

Overcoming the limitations of Vector RAG by understanding entity relationships and global context.

1. Traditional RAG vs GraphRAG

Traditional Vector RAG

Great at finding specific facts ("What is the CEO's name?"). Fails at connecting dots across multiple documents or understanding overarching themes.

GraphRAG

Uses an LLM to build a Knowledge Graph of Entities (Nodes) and Relationships (Edges) from your data. Great at global questions ("What is the overall sentiment of these 50 reports?").

2. The Knowledge Graph Structure

graph TD Company[Microsoft]:::node Person[Satya Nadella]:::node Product[Azure]:::node Person -- "is CEO of" --> Company Company -- "owns" --> Product classDef node fill:#10b981,stroke:#047857,color:#fff;
  • Entities (Nodes): Nouns extracted from text (People, Places, Organizations).
  • Relationships (Edges): Verbs connecting the nouns (owns, works_for, located_in).
  • Ontologies: The strict schema defining what types of entities and relationships are allowed.
